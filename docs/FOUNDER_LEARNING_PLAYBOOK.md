# Founder Learning Playbook

This is the practical learning path for becoming the business owner of PropFlow while the product is being built.

## Your Job As Founder

Your job is not to know everything. Your job is to:

- understand the customer
- sell the product
- protect cash
- hire carefully
- keep the team focused
- make decisions from evidence
- build repeatable systems

## Weekly Founder Routine

Do these every week.

### 1. Talk To Customers

Speak to at least 5 letting agents, property managers, landlords, or contractors.

Ask:

- How do tenants report repairs now?
- Where do documents get lost?
- What compliance dates worry you?
- What do you currently use?
- What do you hate about it?
- Who decides if you buy software?
- What would make you pay monthly?

Write every answer down.

### 2. Demo The Product

Show the app even before it is perfect.

Watch where people get confused, excited, or bored.

Good signs:

- They ask pricing questions.
- They ask when they can use it.
- They describe a real staff member who needs it.
- They offer to send sample spreadsheets.

Bad signs:

- They only say "looks nice".
- They never mention money.
- They do not have the problem often.

### 3. Track The Numbers

At the beginning, track:

- leads contacted
- demos booked
- demos completed
- trial users
- paying agencies
- monthly recurring revenue
- churn
- support issues
- product requests

Later, track:

- CAC: customer acquisition cost
- LTV: lifetime value
- gross margin
- activation rate
- time to onboard an agency
- average properties per agency

## What To Learn First

### Product

Learn how to write:

- user stories
- acceptance criteria
- feature priorities
- simple product roadmaps

Example:

```text
As a property manager, I want to upload an EICR certificate and have the system remind me before it expires, so I do not miss a compliance deadline.
```

### Sales

Learn:

- cold email
- phone outreach
- demo calls
- follow-up
- pricing
- objection handling
- closing

Founder rule:

```text
If you cannot sell it manually, automation will not save it.
```

### Hiring Developers

Learn enough to ask good questions.

You should understand:

- frontend
- backend
- database
- APIs
- authentication
- file storage
- cloud hosting
- mobile apps
- security basics

Hiring process:

1. Write a clear paid test task.
2. Give a small real feature.
3. Judge communication, quality, and reliability.
4. Avoid hiring only from cheapness.
5. Never give one person total control of accounts, code, and infrastructure.

### Operations

Create repeatable processes for:

- onboarding a new agency
- importing spreadsheets
- training staff
- handling support tickets
- reporting bugs
- requesting features
- renewing certificates
- cancelling accounts

## Early Roles To Hire

When cash allows:

1. Full-stack developer
2. Product/UI designer part-time
3. QA tester part-time
4. Customer support/onboarding assistant
5. Salesperson only after you prove the sales script yourself

## Cash Discipline

Do not spend like a funded company before you have reliable revenue.

Protect:

- runway
- server costs
- developer costs
- legal costs
- customer support capacity

Good early spending:

- product development
- customer interviews
- legal templates
- security basics
- onboarding materials

Dangerous early spending:

- expensive office
- big marketing agency
- too many features before sales
- mobile apps before SaaS backend

## Legal And Trust

Because PropFlow stores tenant and property documents, take trust seriously.

You will eventually need:

- privacy policy
- terms of service
- data processing agreement
- GDPR process
- secure document storage
- audit trail
- backup policy
- incident response plan

Use a solicitor before taking serious customer data.

## Decision Rule

When unsure what to build next, ask:

```text
Will this help agencies save time, reduce compliance risk, or improve tenant repair handling?
```

If the answer is no, it can probably wait.
